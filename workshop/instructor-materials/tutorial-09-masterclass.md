# Tutorial 09 master class — 210 minutes

## Block 1 — Biological and mechanical framing, 30 min

- distinguish growth, remodeling, turnover, reorientation, recruitment, and damage;
- introduce Taber, Lanir, Holzapfel–Gasser–Ogden, Driessen, Kuhl, and Humphrey–Rajagopal;
- map biological mechanisms to internal variables.

## Block 2 — Axial statistics and direct reorientation, 35 min

- axial periodicity and doubled-angle statistics;
- implement the first-order rotation law;
- demonstrate the orthogonal stationary case and principal-direction degeneracy.

## Block 3 — Structural constitutive comparison, 40 min

- Lanir angular integration;
- discrete HGO-type families;
- generalized structure tensor and dispersion;
- quadrature convergence and multimodality.

## Break — 10 min

## Block 4 — Continuous ODF remodeling, 35 min

- derive drift–diffusion in orientation space;
- verify normalization and positivity;
- explore the alignment–diffusion map.

## Block 5 — Recruitment and turnover, 35 min

- slack-stretch distributions;
- cohort production, survival, and deposition stretch;
- direct rotation versus replacement.

## Block 6 — Identifiability and research design, 25 min

- fit one loading mode and test another;
- list additional imaging, biochemical, and mechanical observables;
- formulate a tissue-specific research question.
