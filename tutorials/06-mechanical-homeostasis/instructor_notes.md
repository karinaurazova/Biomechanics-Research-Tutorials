# Instructor notes — Tutorial 06

Recommended duration: 180 minutes. This module is intentionally longer than the foundation tutorials because it connects dynamical systems, mechanobiology, turnover, and vascular mechanics.

## Suggested sequence

1. **Definitions and feedback diagram — 20 min.** Ask learners to distinguish set-point, steady state, and attractor.
2. **Analytical scalar model — 25 min.** Derive the exact solution and eigenvalue.
3. **Numerical verification — 20 min.** Compare exact and Euler solutions; discuss metrics.
4. **Nonlinear sensing and response — 30 min.** Dead zone, saturation, noise, filter, bias.
5. **Delay and stability — 30 min.** Interpret the gain–delay map without calling every slow trajectory unstable.
6. **Turnover and constituents — 25 min.** Emphasize nonzero balanced fluxes and cohort memory.
7. **Vascular example — 20 min.** Derive radius and thickness equilibrium.
8. **Research discussion — 10 min.** Identify what must be added for finite growth and constrained mixtures.

## Common misconceptions

- Homeostasis does not mean all variables are constant.
- A steady state is not automatically stable or healthy.
- Zero net mass change does not mean zero turnover.
- A good fit of one trajectory does not identify the biological mechanism.
- A delay is not equivalent to a small adaptation gain.
- The vascular model is a scaling model, not a patient-specific artery.
