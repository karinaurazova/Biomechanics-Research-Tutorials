# 10 — References and further reading

The complete machine-readable bibliography is stored in [`references.bib`](../references.bib).

Primary sources represented in the tutorial include:

- Mooney and Rivlin for invariant-based rubber elasticity;
- Yeoh for a first-invariant polynomial;
- Ogden for principal-stretch energies;
- Gent and Arruda–Boyce for limiting-chain behaviour;
- Veronda and Westmann and Demiray for exponential soft-tissue energies;
- Gasser, Ogden, and Holzapfel for distributed collagen orientations;
- Guccione, Costa, and Holzapfel–Ogden for passive myocardium.

Recommended next steps:

- derive the complete stress tensor for one selected model;
- fit synthetic multi-mode data with uncertainty estimates;
- study parameter correlations and profile likelihoods;
- implement compressible and incompressible finite-element versions;
- compare automatic differentiation with finite differences;
- investigate mathematical restrictions such as ellipticity and polyconvexity.
