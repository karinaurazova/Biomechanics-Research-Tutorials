# Thermodynamic interpretation

For isotropic growth,

\[
\mathbf L_g=\frac{\dot{\ln J_g}}{3}\mathbf I,
\]

so the Mandel contribution is

\[
\mathbf M:\mathbf L_g
=\frac{\dot{\ln J_g}}{3}\operatorname{tr}\mathbf M.
\]

This quantity is reported as a diagnostic, not as a complete dissipation proof. Growth is an open-system process supplied by metabolism, mass transport and chemical reactions. A law that drives stress toward a non-zero homeostatic target need not monotonically minimize stored elastic energy.

A thermodynamically complete theory would specify free energy, mass supply, chemical potential, entropy production and the work associated with growth. The present module instead teaches where these questions enter and prevents a stress-feedback rule from being mislabeled as fully thermodynamic.
