# Boundary control is part of the model

Under prescribed deformation, growth changes \(\mathbf F_e\) and can relax the stress stimulus. Under ideal prescribed stress, the external controller holds the stimulus fixed; growth changes the total size instead of restoring the stress.

For isotropic stress control, the elastic stretch \(\lambda_e\) is found from the nonlinear Cauchy-stress relation. The total stretch is then

\[
\lambda=J_g^{1/3}\lambda_e.
\]

If the imposed stress remains above the homeostatic target, a purely stress-driven law may continue to increase \(J_g\) indefinitely until a bound is reached. This is not necessarily a numerical defect. It demonstrates that the load-control protocol and geometry must close the feedback loop.

Consequently, conclusions from displacement-controlled tests cannot be transferred automatically to force-controlled or pressure-controlled organs.
