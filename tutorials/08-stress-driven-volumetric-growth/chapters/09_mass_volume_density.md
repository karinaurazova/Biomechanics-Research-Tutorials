# Mass, volume, and density bookkeeping

Volumetric growth is frequently interpreted as mass addition at constant density. Under that assumption,

\[
\frac{m}{m_0}=J_g.
\]

More generally,

\[
\frac{m}{m_0}=\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}J_g.
\]

Thus a measured volume change does not uniquely determine mass change unless density is known or assumed. Constant mass with changing \(J_g\) implies a density change, while simultaneous mass and density changes require an additional balance law.

The kinematic variable \(J_g\) is therefore not automatically a biochemical mass variable. A serious model must state whether density is constant, whether source terms are included, and how constituent turnover connects to the natural-volume change.
