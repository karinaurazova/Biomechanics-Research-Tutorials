# Parameter identifiability

A single growth trajectory does not necessarily identify the adaptation rate and homeostatic target independently. A faster law with a shifted target can mimic part of a slower trajectory over a finite observation window.

The tutorial generates a synthetic reference trajectory and maps the root-mean-square error over a grid of rate and target values. The resulting valley illustrates parameter compensation. Better identification requires multiple loading protocols, direct stress information, unloading data or independent constraints on the target.

This is structural insight rather than statistical validation. No measurement noise model or Bayesian posterior is claimed. The exercise prepares the learner for the uncertainty and sensitivity module later in the repository.
