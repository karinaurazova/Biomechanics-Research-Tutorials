# Homogenized constrained-mixture reduction

A homogenized model replaces the explicit cohort list by a small set of averaged internal variables, such as total mass and mean natural stretch. This can reduce state count from order Ntime × Nconstituents to order Nconstituents.

The reduction is accurate when cohort properties do not vary strongly and the chosen moments capture the stress history. It can fail under abrupt loading changes, broad age distributions, nonlinear recruitment, damage, or strongly time-dependent deposition stretch.

The tutorial compares trajectories and quantifies the error rather than treating the reduction as automatically equivalent.
