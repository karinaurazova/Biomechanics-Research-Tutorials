# Axial-dominated loading

An axial-dominated protocol preferentially stretches the primary family. It can represent longitudinal organ growth, tendon loading, uniaxial tissue conditioning, or another directionally biased perturbation.

The model separates adaptation of the primary family from secondary changes in collagen-like families and matrix. The endpoint depends on turnover time, feedback gains, and deposition prestretch.

The comparison with transverse-dominated loading illustrates that the same material can follow different remodeling trajectories under different loading paths.
