# Constrained-mixture hypotheses

A constrained mixture assumes that constituents share the observable motion of the mixture while retaining constituent-specific natural configurations, constitutive responses, production rates, removal rates, and survival functions. This is a modelling hypothesis, not a universal biological law.

The common-motion assumption is often reasonable when phases are mechanically bonded on the continuum scale. It can fail when constituents slide, detach, migrate, swell independently, or exchange fluid rapidly. The tutorial therefore treats the hypothesis as a scale-dependent closure and lists situations in which a multiphase or poromechanical theory may be required.

The mixture stress is assembled from constituent contributions. Mass fractions, reference-volume conventions, and energy normalizations must be stated consistently; otherwise the same symbols can hide different physical meanings.
