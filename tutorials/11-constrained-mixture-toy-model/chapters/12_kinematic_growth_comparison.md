# Comparison with kinematic growth

Kinematic growth represents an evolving natural geometry, whereas a constrained mixture represents production, survival, and constituent-specific natural states. Neither formulation is universally superior.

Kinematic growth is compact and effective for geometry-dominated questions. Constrained mixtures are advantageous when composition, turnover, age, or deposition history are central observables. Hybrid models are possible but require careful avoidance of double-counting.

The tutorial compares state variables, computational cost, identifiable quantities, and the meaning of reversal in both approaches.
