# Production, survival, and mass balance

For constituent α, the surviving mass combines initial material and all subsequently deposited cohorts. In discrete time, production, survival, and removal must satisfy a transparent mass balance. The tutorial verifies that zero net mass change can coexist with nonzero production and removal.

Survival functions encode age-dependent loss. Exponential survival implies a constant hazard; Weibull-type alternatives allow early protection or age-related vulnerability. The choice affects mechanical memory even when the same half-life is reported.

Mass data alone do not identify turnover fluxes. A steady mass can represent rapid replacement or slow replacement, motivating pulse–chase, isotope, biomarker, or longitudinal observations.
