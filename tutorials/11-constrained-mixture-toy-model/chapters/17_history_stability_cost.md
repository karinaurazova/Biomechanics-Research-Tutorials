# History dependence, stability, and computational cost

Mechanobiological feedback introduces slow dynamical systems whose stability depends on gains, delays, turnover times, and mechanical control. The tutorial maps monotone recovery, oscillation, drift, and bounded adaptation.

History dependence also appears numerically: smaller time steps create more cohorts, while aggressive truncation may bias stress. Computational cost must therefore be assessed together with accuracy.

Stable numerical integration does not prove mechanobiological stability, and a stable biological equilibrium does not guarantee a robust discretization.
