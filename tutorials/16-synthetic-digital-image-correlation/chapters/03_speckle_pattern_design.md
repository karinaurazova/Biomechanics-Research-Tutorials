# Speckle-pattern design

A useful pattern must contain spatial intensity gradients in several directions, remain attached to the surface, and preserve contrast throughout loading. Speckle size, density, edge sharpness, dynamic range, and subset size are coupled design choices. A subset that contains too little unique information can translate to several locations with nearly identical correlation.

Synthetic patterns provide controlled tests of these relationships. The generator varies center density, radius distribution, contrast, blur, and random seed. The examples also include multiscale natural-texture surrogates to demonstrate why marker-free analysis can work in some regions and fail in others.
