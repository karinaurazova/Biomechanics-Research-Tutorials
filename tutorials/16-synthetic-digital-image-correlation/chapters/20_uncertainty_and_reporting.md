# Uncertainty and reporting

Uncertainty has random and systematic components. Random components can be estimated from repeated images or noise models. Systematic components arise from calibration, interpolation, lens distortion, out-of-plane motion, subset bias, and strain filtering.

Reports should include camera and lens settings, pixel scale, acquisition rate, pattern preparation, subset and step size, shape function, correlation criterion, interpolation, strain window, masks, thresholds, missing-data handling, coordinate convention, and verification results.
