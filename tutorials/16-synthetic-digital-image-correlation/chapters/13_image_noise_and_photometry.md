# Noise, blur, and photometric change

Sensor noise, optical blur, compression, illumination drift, and surface reflectivity changes alter the correlation surface. ZNCC reduces ideal gain and offset sensitivity, but it does not correct nonlinear intensity changes, saturation, shadows, or evolving surface texture.

Synthetic sweeps distinguish two consequences: increasing displacement error and decreasing valid coverage. Reporting only the surviving high-correlation points can hide selection bias.
