# From displacement to strain

Displacement is interpolated or fitted before spatial differentiation. The infinitesimal strain tensor uses the symmetric part of the displacement gradient. The Green–Lagrange tensor includes quadratic gradient terms and remains appropriate for finite rotations and stretches within a reference description.

Differentiation amplifies noise. Reported strain therefore depends strongly on the smoothing or fitting window. A strain map without the displacement processing choices, spatial resolution, and uncertainty is incomplete.
