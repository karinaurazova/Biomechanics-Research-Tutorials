# Stereo-DIC and digital volume correlation

Two-dimensional DIC assumes a planar surface, in-plane motion, and a stable camera. Stereo-DIC uses calibrated cameras to reconstruct surface shape and all three displacement components. Digital volume correlation extends the correlation concept to volumetric subsets from CT, MRI, confocal, or other 3D imaging.

These methods require additional calibration, correspondence, interpolation, and uncertainty analysis. Tutorial 16 implements only the 2D baseline but defines the conceptual bridge to later multimodal and volumetric modules.
