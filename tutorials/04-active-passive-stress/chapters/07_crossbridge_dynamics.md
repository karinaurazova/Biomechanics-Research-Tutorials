# Calcium and cross-bridge dynamics\n\nA minimal two-state model is used:\n\n\[\dot c=(c_{target}-c)/\tau_c,\]\n\n\[\dot x=k_{on}c^n(1-x)-k_{off}x.\]\n\nThe filtered calcium state `c` responds to the prescribed transient; `x` is the bound cross-bridge fraction. The model produces delay and relaxation without pretending to reproduce a full sarcomere. It is useful for teaching internal variables, explicit time integration, bounds, and identifiability.
