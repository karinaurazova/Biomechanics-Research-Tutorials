# Active strain\n\nActive strain introduces a multiplicative decomposition\n\n\[\mathbf F=\mathbf F_e\mathbf F_a,\qquad \mathbf F_e=\mathbf F\mathbf F_a^{-1}.\]\n\nFor contraction along `a0`, the tutorial uses\n\n\[\mathbf F_a=\lambda_a\mathbf a_0\otimes\mathbf a_0+\lambda_a^{-1/2}(\mathbf I-\mathbf a_0\otimes\mathbf a_0),\]\n\nso `det(Fa)=1`. Passive energy is evaluated on `Fe`. Active strain therefore changes the elastic state seen by the passive material and can alter multiaxial coupling even when a uniaxial point is matched to an active-stress model.
