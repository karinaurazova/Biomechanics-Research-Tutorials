# Boundary conditions

Boundary conditions are treated as part of the learning problem. The left end has a prescribed displacement. The right end has a prescribed traction. These two constraints remove rigid-body ambiguity and fix the stress scale.

This chapter explains why boundary conditions are often the weakest link in experimental mechanics. If the load path, contact condition or clamp compliance is wrong, a physics-informed model may fit an incorrect physical problem very confidently.

The tutorial therefore reports boundary errors separately from displacement and residual errors.
