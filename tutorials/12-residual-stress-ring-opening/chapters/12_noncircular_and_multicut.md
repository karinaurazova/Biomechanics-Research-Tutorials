# Noncircular opening and multiple cuts

Real specimens need not open as perfect circular sectors. Spatially varying residual strain can produce asymmetric arcs, multiple equilibrium shapes, or local curling.

A single opening angle then becomes a lossy summary. The tutorial generates multi-sector fields and compares radial, axial, layer-separation, and grid-cut protocols by the information they release.

Full-field contour tracking and local curvature can retain more information than one endpoint angle, but they also require more careful registration and uncertainty analysis.
