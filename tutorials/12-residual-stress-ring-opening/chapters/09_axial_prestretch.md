# Axial prestretch and three-dimensional coupling

Closing a sector is not purely circumferential. Incompressibility couples circumferential closure to radial and axial stretches. Axial prestretch changes wall thickness, pressure response, and inferred residual stress.

Experiments show that longitudinal residual deformation may be substantial even when a radial cut is emphasized. Ring opening alone therefore cannot recover the full three-dimensional prestress state.

The tutorial sweeps axial stretch and compares stress profiles, motivating combined ring, strip, and force measurements.
