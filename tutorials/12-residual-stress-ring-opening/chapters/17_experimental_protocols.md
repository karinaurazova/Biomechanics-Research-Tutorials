# Experimental protocols and measurement practice

A robust protocol records specimen orientation, temperature, hydration, active-state suppression, time after excision, unloaded dimensions, cut order, image scale, and the definition used for opening angle.

Useful protocols include radial ring cuts, axial strips, layer separation, multiple radial cuts, grid cuts, pressure–diameter testing, axial force measurement, and full-field optical tracking. Repeated measurements estimate biological and operator variability.

The tutorial includes a measurement-uncertainty Monte Carlo model and shows how angular uncertainty propagates into inferred closure factors and stress.
