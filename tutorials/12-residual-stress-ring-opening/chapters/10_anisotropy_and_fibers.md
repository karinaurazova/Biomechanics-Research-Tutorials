# Anisotropy and fiber reinforcement

Fiber orientation and recruitment alter the relation between opening geometry and stress. Circumferential reinforcement carries load only after its elastic stretch exceeds the slack threshold in the teaching model.

Distributed fibers, crossed families, and layer-specific orientations can produce similar opening angles but different loaded stress fields. This is one reason why imaging-informed structural priors are useful.

The tutorial compares isotropic and reinforced walls while keeping geometry fixed, isolating constitutive-model uncertainty.
