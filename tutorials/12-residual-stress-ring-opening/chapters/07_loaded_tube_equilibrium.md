# Loaded tube equilibrium

For an axisymmetric tube, radial equilibrium is

\[
\frac{d\sigma_r}{dr}+\frac{\sigma_r-\sigma_\theta}{r}=0.
\]

Boundary conditions prescribe inner and outer radial tractions. Integrating the stress difference through the wall determines pressure and the radial stress field.

The tutorial verifies inner and outer traction residuals, incompressibility, and mesh convergence. A correct residual-stress model must satisfy equilibrium in both unloaded and loaded states.
