# Open-sector kinematics

The classical opening-angle model assumes that a stress-free annular sector closes into a circular ring. If α is the missing wedge, the angular closure factor is

\[
\kappa=\frac{2\pi}{2\pi-\alpha}.
\]

For incompressible axisymmetric closure with axial stretch λz, radial and circumferential stretches satisfy λr λθ λz = 1. The tutorial derives the mapping between sector radius R and ring radius r and verifies the Jacobian numerically.

This reduction is powerful but restrictive: it assumes circular geometry, axisymmetry, uniform opening angle, and a chosen axial constraint.
