# Multilayer tissues and layer separation

Many tubular tissues contain mechanically distinct layers. Each layer may have its own stress-free sector, stiffness, fiber orientation, swelling, and turnover history.

Forcing different layers into one intact ring creates interfacial compatibility constraints. When layers are separated, each can open or curl differently, revealing hidden mismatch that a whole-wall angle averages out.

The tutorial implements two-layer closure and layer separation. Interface traction continuity and geometry compatibility are checked explicitly.
