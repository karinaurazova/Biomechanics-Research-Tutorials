# Stress redistribution and homogenization

A central hypothesis in vascular mechanics is that residual stress can reduce through-thickness gradients of working circumferential stress. The tutorial tests this statement rather than assuming it.

For the synthetic parameter set, an intermediate opening angle reduces the coefficient of variation under pressure, whereas too little or too much opening can increase nonuniformity. The optimum depends on pressure, geometry, material anisotropy, and axial prestretch.

Stress homogenization is therefore a conditional mechanical outcome, not a universal definition of biological optimality.
