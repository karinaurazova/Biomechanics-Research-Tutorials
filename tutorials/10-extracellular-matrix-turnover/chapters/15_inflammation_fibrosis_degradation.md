# Inflammation, fibrosis, and degradative remodeling

Inflammation affects both production and removal. Cytokine signaling can stimulate fibroblast activity and collagen synthesis while MMP activation accelerates degradation. The net result depends on timing, magnitude, inhibitor response, and maturation.

Tutorial 10 compares three synthetic regimes: homeostatic turnover, fibrotic remodeling with elevated synthesis and cross-link formation, and degradative remodeling with elevated enzyme activity. These regimes are not diagnoses and use no clinical calibration.

A transient inflammatory episode can leave a long-lived mechanical memory because lost mature collagen and cross-links recover slowly. Thus, the inflammatory signal may return to baseline before stiffness does.

The model also demonstrates why “fibrosis = more collagen” is incomplete. Cross-link density, orientation, maturity, and deposition prestretch can dominate mechanical consequences.
