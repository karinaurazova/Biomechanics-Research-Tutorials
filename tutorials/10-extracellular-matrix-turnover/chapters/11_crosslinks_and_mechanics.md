# Cross-links, architecture, and mechanics

Cross-links modify load transfer between molecules and fibrils, influence stiffness and failure, and may alter susceptibility to degradation. Tutorial 10 evolves a bounded cross-link fraction $x\in[0,1]$:

$$\dot x=k_x\phi(C_m)(1-x)-k_{xl}a_ex.$$

The effective collagen stiffness is represented by

$$K_{\mathrm{eff}}=K_0C_m(1+g_xx).$$

This expression is phenomenological but exposes a critical point: collagen mass and mechanical quality are separate states. Fibrosis can involve both excess collagen and increased cross-linking; degradation can reduce mass while surviving cross-linked fibers maintain substantial stiffness.

The tutorial refers to Holzapfel–Ogden-type microstructural thinking when discussing how collagen architecture and cross-links enter constitutive response. It does not claim that one scalar cross-link fraction reproduces the full three-dimensional network.
