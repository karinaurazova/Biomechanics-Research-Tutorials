# Mechanobiological feedback and loading history

Mechanically regulated turnover requires an explicit stimulus, target, and response law. Tutorial 10 uses a normalized stress error and separate gains for production and degradation. A dead zone and saturation prevent unbounded responses.

The model distinguishes sustained overload, transient overload, and unloading. Because synthesis, maturation, degradation, and cross-linking have different timescales, equal peak stresses can generate different outcomes depending on duration.

Taber-style growth and remodeling perspectives motivate separating mechanical cues associated with pressure, flow, fiber stress, and cellular traction. A single scalar stress should not be assumed to regulate every ECM component identically.

Mechanotransduction is not represented mechanistically here. The response law is a transparent bridge between mechanics and biology that can later be replaced by signaling-network or gene-expression models.
