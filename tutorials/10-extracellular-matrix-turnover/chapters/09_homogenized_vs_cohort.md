# Homogenized versus explicit-cohort models

When survival is exponential and all deposited material has identical properties, explicit cohort history can be reduced to

$$\dot m=p(t)-\lambda m.$$

Tutorial 10 verifies this equivalence numerically. The homogenized equation is efficient and often adequate for total mass, but it does not automatically preserve age distribution, deposition prestretch history, or nonlinear cohort-specific mechanics.

A homogenized constrained-mixture model can recover more of this information by evolving additional internal variables. Nevertheless, every reduction should be tested against an explicit-history reference under the loading protocols of interest.

The choice is therefore not “simple versus correct.” It is a hierarchy of approximations. The appropriate level depends on observables, timescales, and whether history-dependent prestress is central to the research question.

## Key modeling checkpoint

State explicitly which quantities are conserved, which are constitutive assumptions, and which are experimentally observable.
