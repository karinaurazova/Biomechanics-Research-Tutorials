# Deposition stretch and prestress

A newly deposited constituent may enter the tissue with a preferred prestretch. In constrained-mixture notation, this deposition stretch determines the elastic state of the cohort immediately after deposition.

The reduced fiber law uses an elastic stretch

$$\lambda_e=\lambda\,G_h,$$

where $G_h$ is the deposition stretch parameter. If $G_h>1$, a cohort can carry tension even when the observed total stretch is one.

Changing deposition stretch shifts recruitment, prestress, and the load shared by old and new material. It is therefore not interchangeable with changing stiffness or mass.

Deposition stretch is difficult to infer from endpoint geometry alone. Mechanical testing, opening-angle or residual-stress data, constituent-specific imaging, and multiple loading modes may be required.
