# Instructor notes — Tutorial 10

The main teaching goal is to separate **stock**, **flux**, **age structure**, and **mechanical quality**.

Key interventions:

1. Ask learners whether constant collagen mass implies zero turnover. It does not.
2. Require every model to state its reference volume and units.
3. Compare an exponential survival law with an age-dependent Weibull law.
4. Show that explicit cohorts and a first-order ODE agree only under restrictive assumptions.
5. Keep collagen mass, cross-link fraction, and deposition stretch as separate variables.
6. Do not present MMP abundance as identical to collagenolytic activity.
7. Treat fibrosis and matrix degradation as mechanisms, not diagnostic predictions.
8. Require a measurement operator when mapping model states to assays.

The synthetic benchmark verifies implementation properties, not biological truth. Encourage learners to design the minimal multimodal experiment that breaks the production–removal compensation.
