# Research Challenge

Extend the model by changing one scientific assumption.

Possible directions:

- make the rate depend on stimulus magnitude;
- introduce a threshold below which remodeling stops;
- add a finite response delay;
- represent a distribution of orientations;
- compute the target from a two-dimensional stress tensor;
- compare stress-driven and strain-driven targets;
- add a stochastic branch-selection rule near 90 degrees;
- distinguish elastic rotation from structural remodeling.

## Required structure

1. Scientific hypothesis
2. Connection to at least one primary research paper
3. Modified equation
4. Parameter definitions and units
5. Numerical implementation
6. Verification case
7. Computational experiment
8. Interpretation
9. Limitations
10. Proposed validation data
11. Reproducibility statement

## Assessment rubric

| Criterion | Weight |
|---|---:|
| Scientific clarity | 20% |
| Mathematical consistency | 20% |
| Verification quality | 20% |
| Reproducibility | 15% |
| Interpretation and limitations | 15% |
| Communication | 10% |
