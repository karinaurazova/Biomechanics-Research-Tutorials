# Forward and inverse imaging problems

The forward problem maps a latent structure $x$ to an observation $y$: $y=\mathcal H(x;\eta)+arepsilon$, where $\eta$ denotes instrument parameters and $arepsilon$ denotes noise. The inverse problem estimates structure or parameters from $y$. Synthetic data are valuable because $x$, $\eta$, and the exact perturbations are known, allowing method error and observation error to be quantified separately.
