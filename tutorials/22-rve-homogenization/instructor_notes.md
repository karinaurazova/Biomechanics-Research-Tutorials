# Instructor notes — Tutorial 22

This tutorial is best taught after students have completed Tutorial 21.  The key pedagogical goal is to show that homogenization is a boundary-value problem, not a decorative post-processing step.

Recommended teaching sequence:

1. Start from Voigt and Reuss bounds because they are easy to understand.
2. Introduce the affine-plus-periodic displacement decomposition.
3. Show the reduction matrix and periodic node classes.
4. Run one macroscopic strain case and inspect local strain/stress maps.
5. Assemble the effective stiffness from three cases.
6. Discuss Hill--Mandel consistency before discussing biological interpretation.

Common confusion: students often expect the effective tensor to be aligned exactly with the average fiber orientation.  The tutorial helps show why porosity, connectivity, and boundary relaxation can rotate or weaken apparent anisotropy.
