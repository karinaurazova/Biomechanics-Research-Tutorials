# 10. Recruitment, crimp, and cross-link state

Observed collagen remodeling is not limited to orientation. Wavy or crimped fibers become load-bearing only after their slack stretch is exceeded. A distribution of recruitment stretches creates the characteristic toe region of many soft-tissue stress–stretch curves.

The tutorial represents recruitment by a probability distribution of slack stretches. The recruited fraction is a cumulative distribution, and total tension is obtained by integrating the post-recruitment response over that distribution.

Changes in the toe region can arise from:

- altered mean slack stretch;
- altered width of the slack-stretch distribution;
- changes in cross-link density or maturation;
- changes in fiber mass;
- changes in orientation relative to loading.

These mechanisms can compensate each other. A stiffer measured curve does not uniquely imply increased collagen mass. It may reflect earlier recruitment, reduced dispersion, increased prestretch, or altered cross-linking. This observation is central to experimental design and parameter identification.
