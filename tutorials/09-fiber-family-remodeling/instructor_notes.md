# Instructor notes

## Teaching objective

The central teaching objective is to prevent learners from using the word *remodeling* for mathematically different mechanisms. Require every group to state:

1. which material is changing;
2. which internal variable carries that change;
3. whether existing material rotates or is replaced;
4. which mechanical cue is used;
5. how the cue is regularized near degeneracy;
6. which data could identify the model.

## Common misconceptions

- A principal direction is always unique.
- Mean orientation and dispersion uniquely determine the distribution.
- A GOH dispersion parameter is an experimental observable independent of model assumptions.
- A final aligned architecture proves that fibers physically rotated.
- Increased stiffness proves increased collagen mass.
- A direct reorientation law is equivalent to a constrained-mixture turnover model.
- Loaded-image angle changes are automatically biological remodeling.

## Recommended emphasis

Spend substantial time on the direct-rotation versus cohort-replacement comparison. The two models can be calibrated to similar endpoint angles, yet only the turnover model changes mass, cohort age, and deposition history. Use this to introduce hidden-state identifiability.

## Assessment prompts

Ask learners to defend a model choice for tendon, arterial wall, heart valve, scar, and myocardium. The answer should differ across tissues and available measurements.
