# Scope and synthetic-data philosophy

Tutorial 13 builds controlled fibrous geometries whose hidden state is known exactly. The purpose is not to imitate one specimen photorealistically. The purpose is to create falsifiable test cases for segmentation, orientation extraction, topology analysis, image formation, inverse mechanics, and uncertainty studies.

A synthetic sample consists of more than an image. It includes the discrete fibers, family labels, radii, local tangent directions, a binary mask, overlap count, thickness field, skeleton, topology metrics, random seed, and generator parameters. This separation is essential: an image is an observation of a structure, whereas the structure itself is the object that later imaging tutorials will perturb.

Every preset is therefore an educational analogue. A tendon-like bundle or vessel-wall-like laminate captures selected architectural features but is not a calibrated replica of a species, organ, disease, or manufacturing process. Synthetic verification can show whether an algorithm recovers known truth; it cannot establish experimental validity.
