# Layers and spatial orientation gradients

Layered generators assign different preferred axes to separate depth intervals. Gradient generators create curved fibers whose tangent rotates across the domain. Both cases require field-valued ground truth rather than one global orientation.

A layer label is not the same quantity as a fiber-family label. Layers describe spatial regions; families describe structural populations. The current examples use compatible integer labels, but the conceptual distinction is maintained in the documentation.

These generators support later studies of transmural rotation, interfacial smoothing, registration error, and spatial regularization.
