# Cellular and Voronoi-like architectures

Voronoi ridges provide a second network family with closed or nearly closed cells. It differs fundamentally from random rod deposition: connectivity is generated by tessellation rather than incidental intersections.

The implementation adds guard points so that central ridges are finite and keeps segments inside a padded domain. It is a geometric generator, not a physical model of alveoli, foam, or trabecular tissue.

Comparing Mikado and Voronoi architectures at similar area fraction demonstrates why porosity alone cannot determine mechanics or transport.
