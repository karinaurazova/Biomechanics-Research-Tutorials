# Axial orientation statistics

An ordinary arithmetic mean fails near the axial boundary: +89° and -89° represent almost the same axis but average to 0° arithmetically. The correct mean uses the second trigonometric moment. The same moment defines an order parameter between zero and one.

The axial von Mises sampler draws doubled angles and divides them by two. Its concentration parameter controls dispersion but does not change the prescribed mean axis. Low concentration produces nearly isotropic networks; high concentration produces aligned bundles.

Crossing families expose the limitation of a single local orientation. At overlap pixels the two-family ground truth remains available through family labels and overlap count, while the single-axis coherence decreases.
