# Mikado and deposited-line networks

Mikado networks are generated by depositing finite rods with random centres, lengths, radii, and axial orientations. They are useful minimal models of disordered fibrous media because density, alignment, and length distributions can be controlled independently.

In the present module, intersections do not automatically create beam joints. The raster mask may be connected at an intersection, but a future mechanics model must decide whether fibers are bonded, freely crossing, sliding, or only optically overlapping.

Increasing fiber count changes area fraction, component size, and graph topology at the same time. Consequently, density should never be interpreted through porosity alone.
