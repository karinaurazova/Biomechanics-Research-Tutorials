# Experiment

## X1 — Loading-relative dispersion

At $\lambda=1.2$, compute nominal stress for mean angles from 0 to 90 degrees and concentrations from 0 to 16. Identify regions where increasing concentration raises stress and regions where it lowers stress.

Deliverables:

- one heatmap;
- one line plot at a selected mean angle;
- a 150-word interpretation.

## X2 — Fiber compression rule

Replace the positive-part strain with a model in which all fibers contribute symmetrically in tension and compression. Compare the stress response and discuss physical plausibility.

## X3 — Parameter compensation

Find two combinations of concentration and fiber stiffness that produce similar stress at $\lambda=1.15$. Test whether they remain similar across $1\le\lambda\le1.25$.

## X4 — Quadrature budget

Choose the smallest odd quadrature size that keeps the maximum stress error below a tolerance of your choice. Justify the tolerance and report the computational cost.
