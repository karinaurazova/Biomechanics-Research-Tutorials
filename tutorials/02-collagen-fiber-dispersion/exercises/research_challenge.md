# Research Challenge

## Challenge — From orientation image to mechanical prediction

Replace the analytical von Mises density with a normalized synthetic or image-derived orientation histogram.

### Minimum requirements

1. Document how orientations were generated or extracted.
2. Respect axial periodicity.
3. Normalize the discrete density.
4. Compute the second-order orientation tensor.
5. Compare the histogram-based mechanical response with a fitted von Mises approximation.
6. Quantify at least one source of uncertainty.
7. State which differences are caused by distribution shape and which may be numerical.

### Research question examples

- Does a unimodal von Mises fit preserve the nonlinear response of a bimodal distribution?
- How sensitive is stress to errors in mean angle versus errors in dispersion?
- Can two distributions with the same orientation tensor produce different stress–stretch curves?
- What spatial window size is needed for a stable orientation estimate?

### Evidence boundary

Do not label the result experimentally validated unless the image-processing method, mechanical data, registration, units, and uncertainty have all been independently established.
