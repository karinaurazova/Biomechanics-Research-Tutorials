# 6. Computational experiments

## Experiment A — Distribution gallery

Plot the density and a synthetic fiber population for $\beta=0$, 1, 4, and 12 at fixed mean angle. Compare the probability peak, order parameter, and visible alignment.

## Experiment B — Tensor verification

Compute the analytical and numerical orientation tensors over a concentration sweep. Compare their eigenvalues and report the maximum component error.

## Experiment C — Aligned loading

Set $\mu=0^\circ$, parallel to uniaxial extension. Compare stress–stretch curves for several concentrations. A narrow aligned family produces a stronger fiber-direction response than a uniform or broadly dispersed family.

## Experiment D — Oblique loading

Set $\mu=45^\circ$. A highly concentrated family remains oblique, while a dispersed family contains fibers closer to the loading axis. The ordering of stress curves may differ from the aligned case.

## Experiment E — Anisotropy map

At a fixed stretch, vary both mean angle and concentration. Plot nominal stress as a two-dimensional map. This makes clear that concentration cannot be interpreted independently of loading-relative mean direction.

## Experiment F — Quadrature convergence

Compute a dense reference solution and compare angular grids from 33 to 1025 points. Report maximum stress error.

## Experiment G — Synthetic alignment animation

Animate a population as $\beta$ increases while the mean direction remains fixed. The animation visualizes a statistical transition, not a mechanistic remodeling law. No claim is made that real fibers rotate according to this animation.
