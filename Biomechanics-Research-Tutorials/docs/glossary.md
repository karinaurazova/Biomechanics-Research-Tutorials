# Glossary

**Anisotropy**
Dependence of material response on direction.

**Fiber orientation**
The axis associated with a fiber family. For an undirected fiber, angles separated by 180 degrees represent the same orientation.

**Remodeling**
A change in tissue composition, internal structure, or material properties over time.

**Verification**
Checking whether equations and numerical algorithms are implemented correctly.

**Validation**
Assessing whether a model adequately represents a real system for a stated purpose.

**Synthetic benchmark**
A computationally generated test case with known assumptions and expected behavior.


**Orientation distribution**
A probability density describing the relative abundance of fiber axes over angle.

**Mean axial orientation**
The preferred undirected fiber axis. Angles separated by 180 degrees are equivalent.

**Concentration parameter**
A parameter controlling the width of a specified probability distribution. Its meaning and numerical range depend on the chosen distribution.

**Orientation tensor**
A tensorial moment of an orientation distribution used to summarize directional structure. A second-order tensor does not preserve every feature of the complete distribution.

**Angular integration**
Evaluation of a distributed material contribution by integrating orientation-specific responses over an orientation probability density.

**Identifiability**
The ability to determine model parameters uniquely from available observations.

## Residual-stress terms

**Residual stress** — stress remaining after external tractions are removed.

**Residual strain** — deformation released by cutting or separating material.

**Opening angle** — geometric measure of ring release; not itself a stress.

**Initial-stress formulation** — model that uses an already stressed reference geometry without explicitly constructing a stress-free state.
