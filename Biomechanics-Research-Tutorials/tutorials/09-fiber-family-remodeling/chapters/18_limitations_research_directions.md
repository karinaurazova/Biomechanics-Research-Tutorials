# 18. Limitations and research directions

The tutorial is intentionally homogeneous and planar. It does not solve a spatial equilibrium problem, model non-affine fibril kinematics, resolve biochemical pathways, or identify parameters from experimental data.

Further developments include:

- three-dimensional spherical orientation distributions;
- coupled finite-element equilibrium and remodeling;
- separate collagen types and layer-specific families;
- damage, cross-link maturation, and enzymatic degradation;
- constituent-specific deposition stretches and natural configurations;
- nonlocal or gradient regularization of orientation fields;
- coupling to flow, active smooth-muscle tone, inflammation, and signaling;
- Bayesian calibration using imaging and mechanics;
- image-informed initial distributions from polarization microscopy or structure tensors;
- comparison of hereditary constrained mixtures with homogenized internal-variable models.

The principal scientific rule is to match model complexity to observable data. A detailed cohort or distribution model is not automatically more credible if its internal variables cannot be constrained. Conversely, a one-angle model should not be used to make biological claims about synthesis, degradation, or cross-link turnover that it does not represent.
