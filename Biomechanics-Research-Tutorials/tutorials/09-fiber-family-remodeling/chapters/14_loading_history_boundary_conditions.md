# 14. Loading history and boundary conditions

Fiber remodeling is path dependent. A sequence of loading directions generally produces a different internal state than the reversed sequence, even when the final external load is identical.

History dependence enters through:

- finite reorientation time;
- cohort survival and replacement;
- nonlinear recruitment;
- deposition prestretch;
- mass-dependent redistribution of stress;
- delayed or filtered mechanosensing.

Boundary conditions also matter. Under prescribed deformation, increasing fiber mass or alignment can increase reaction forces. Under prescribed force, the same remodeling can reduce deformation. A cue based on stress may therefore evolve differently under load control and displacement control.

The tutorial's homogeneous examples are intentionally simple, but the interpretation carries directly to finite-element problems. Every reported remodeling result should state the loading protocol, control mode, initial architecture, and whether equilibrium is solved after each remodeling increment.
