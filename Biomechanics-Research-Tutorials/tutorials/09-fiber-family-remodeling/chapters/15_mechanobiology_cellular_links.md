# 15. From mechanobiology to internal variables

Continuum variables should be connected to plausible biological processes without claiming one-to-one identification.

Examples include:

- cell traction and contact guidance mapped to a target orientation or drift velocity;
- collagen synthesis mapped to a production rate;
- matrix metalloproteinase activity mapped to survival or removal kinetics;
- cross-link maturation mapped to fiber stiffness or degradation resistance;
- fibril crimp mapped to a slack-stretch distribution;
- integrin and cytoskeletal adaptation mapped to sensing gains, delays, or thresholds;
- phenotype changes mapped to altered production and degradation laws.

Mechanics alone rarely specifies these laws. Biochemical signaling, inflammation, hypoxia, age, disease, and cell phenotype may shift the homeostatic target or modify responsiveness. A serious mechanobiological model should therefore distinguish measured relationships from hypotheses and should expose parameters that require biological calibration.

The biology-to-model map in this tutorial is a bookkeeping tool: it shows where a mechanism would enter the equations and what data would be needed to support it.
