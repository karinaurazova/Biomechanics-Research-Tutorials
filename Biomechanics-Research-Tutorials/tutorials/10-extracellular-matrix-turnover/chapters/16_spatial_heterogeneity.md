# Spatial heterogeneity and transport limitations

ECM turnover is spatially heterogeneous because cells, enzymes, inhibitors, nutrients, and mechanical stimuli vary in space. Tutorial 10 includes a one-dimensional reaction–diffusion model for enzyme activity and collagen loss.

No-flux boundaries are used for teaching. The enzyme field diffuses, decays, and is generated by a localized source; collagen is degraded locally and produced uniformly. The result is a degradation front and a delayed recovery profile.

Spatial smoothing should not be confused with biological transport unless the coefficient has a physical interpretation. Numerical regularization and enzyme diffusion can produce similar visual effects but represent different mechanisms.

A full model would couple transport to deformation, porosity, binding, cellular sources, and anisotropic matrix architecture.

## Key modeling checkpoint

State explicitly which quantities are conserved, which are constitutive assumptions, and which are experimentally observable.
