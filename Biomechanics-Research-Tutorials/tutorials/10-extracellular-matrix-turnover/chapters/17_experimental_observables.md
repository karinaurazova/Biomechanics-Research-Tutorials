# Experimental observables and pulse–chase logic

Different experiments observe different parts of turnover. Total collagen assays estimate stock; stable-isotope or pulse–chase methods inform synthesis and survival; MMP/TIMP assays inform potential degradation; cross-link assays inform maturity; mechanical tests inform integrated load-bearing quality; imaging informs architecture.

The synthetic pulse–chase experiment demonstrates that a labelled fraction constrains survival but does not directly reveal unlabeled production. Likewise, unchanged mass does not imply slow turnover.

The observation map in this tutorial makes explicit that no single modality identifies every mechanism. A defensible study combines orthogonal data types and defines a measurement operator from model states to observables.

For image-informed mechanics, orientation maps should be paired with mass, maturity, and mechanical measurements rather than interpreted as a complete description of ECM remodeling.

## Key modeling checkpoint

State explicitly which quantities are conserved, which are constitutive assumptions, and which are experimentally observable.
