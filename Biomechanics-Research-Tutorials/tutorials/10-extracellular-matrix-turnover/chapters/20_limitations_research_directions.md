# Limitations and research directions

The tutorial omits many processes: cell population dynamics, transcriptional networks, explicit TGF-β signaling, collagen molecular assembly, anisotropic enzyme transport, damage, fiber interactions, poroelastic swelling, and three-dimensional finite-element equilibrium.

The scalar MMP/TIMP proxy cannot reproduce detailed enzyme kinetics. The stiffness law is a reduced phenomenological relation. Spatial degradation is one-dimensional. Constituent interactions are mostly additive.

These limitations are deliberate. The module creates a verified platform for progressively adding complexity while preserving mass balance and interpretability.

Research extensions include coupling to signaling networks, explicit collagen I/III fibrillogenesis, mechanochemical cross-link maturation, stochastic cohort survival, spatial cell–matrix models, image-derived constituent fields, and Bayesian inference from multimodal experiments.
