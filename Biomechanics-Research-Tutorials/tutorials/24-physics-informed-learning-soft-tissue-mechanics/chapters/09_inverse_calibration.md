# Inverse calibration

The inverse experiment calibrates one scale factor multiplying the image-derived stiffness field. This is deliberately modest. It is meant to teach identifiability before moving to larger spatial parameter fields.

If the objective has a clear minimum, the data and physics contain enough information to identify that scale. If the objective is flat, many stiffness scales explain the observations equally well. If the minimum is far from the true value, the constitutive prior or boundary conditions may be biased.

This chapter connects Tutorial 24 back to Tutorial 19, where image-informed constitutive parameters were estimated from structure, DIC and load data.
