# Extensions and research tasks


The first natural extension is finite-strain hyperelastic homogenization.  The imposed macro quantity becomes a deformation gradient, and the RVE returns average first Piola or Cauchy stress.

A second extension is 3-D microstructure.  This is essential for many soft tissues because out-of-plane fiber rotation, sheet structure and pore networks can dominate the response.

A third extension is uncertainty quantification.  Image segmentation, orientation recovery and material calibration all introduce uncertainty.  The effective stiffness should therefore be reported with uncertainty intervals, not only as one deterministic matrix.

A final extension is surrogate modeling.  Once many RVE simulations are available, a surrogate can learn the map from structural descriptors to effective stiffness.  That is exactly why the next tutorial in the roadmap moves toward surrogate modeling.
