# Soft tissue and biological surfaces

Biological surfaces are compliant, wet, reflective, irregular, and often strongly heterogeneous. Applied speckles can detach or alter hydration, while natural texture may evolve during loading. Out-of-plane motion violates planar 2D assumptions.

DIC remains valuable because it provides full-field information where point markers or extensometers miss local heterogeneity. Experimental design should consider tissue preservation, pattern compatibility, loading rate, camera synchronization, and whether stereo-DIC or image registration is required.
