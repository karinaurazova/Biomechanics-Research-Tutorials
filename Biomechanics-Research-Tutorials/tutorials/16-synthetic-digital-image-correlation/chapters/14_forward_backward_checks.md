# Forward–backward consistency

A forward analysis maps the reference to the deformed image. A backward analysis reverses their roles. For an ideal invertible motion, the composed displacement should approximately vanish. Large forward–backward error flags ambiguous or unstable regions.

Consistency is not a ground-truth error. Both directions can agree on the same biased result. It should be combined with correlation quality, residuals, texture diagnostics, synthetic verification, and independent physical checks.
