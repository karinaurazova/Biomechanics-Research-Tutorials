# Verification and good practice

A credible DIC result is supported by a hierarchy of checks: analytical rigid and affine fields, synthetic image deformation, zero-load image pairs, translation tests, sensitivity to analysis parameters, repeated acquisitions, independent displacement scales, and documented calibration.

The benchmark in this tutorial checks translation error, analytical strain, affine refinement, forward–backward consistency, expected noise degradation, and provenance. Passing these checks verifies the educational implementation under specified conditions; it does not validate a camera, specimen preparation, or biological conclusion.
