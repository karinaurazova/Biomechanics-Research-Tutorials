# Discontinuities and damage

A subset that crosses a crack or sliding interface contains more than one displacement branch. A single smooth translation or affine map cannot represent that kinematics. The optimizer may average both sides, follow the dominant texture, or lose correlation completely.

Discontinuous DIC, enriched bases, adaptive masks, phase-field-assisted methods, or separate tracking of each side are needed when cracks are an intended measurement. Standard smoothing must not bridge the discontinuity.
