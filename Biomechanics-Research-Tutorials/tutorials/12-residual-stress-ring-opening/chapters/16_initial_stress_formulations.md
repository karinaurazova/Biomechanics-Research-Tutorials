# Initial-stress and stress-free-reference formulations

Two broad strategies exist. A stress-free-reference approach reconstructs a virtual released configuration and deforms it into the observed body. An initial-stress approach starts from the observed geometry and assigns a self-equilibrated stress field.

The first gives geometric intuition but may require an unrealizable global reference. The second avoids constructing that reference but must satisfy equilibrium, compatibility constraints, constitutive consistency, and often an initial-stress symmetry condition.

The tutorial compares state variables and inverse ambiguity. The two formulations are not interchangeable unless a consistent mapping is established.
