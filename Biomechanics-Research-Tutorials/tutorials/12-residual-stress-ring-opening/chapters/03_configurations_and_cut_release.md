# Configurations and cut release

Three configurations must be distinguished: the intact loaded state, the intact traction-free but residually stressed state, and the locally stress-released cut state. A single globally stress-free configuration may not exist when growth or deposition is incompatible.

Cutting changes boundary conditions and can release only selected components of residual strain. A radial cut in a ring mainly reveals circumferential incompatibility; axial strips reveal longitudinal gradients; layer separation reveals interfacial mismatch; multiple cuts reveal spatial heterogeneity.

The observed released shape depends on cut number, cut location, sample thickness, viscoelastic relaxation, active tone, hydration, temperature, and imaging time.
