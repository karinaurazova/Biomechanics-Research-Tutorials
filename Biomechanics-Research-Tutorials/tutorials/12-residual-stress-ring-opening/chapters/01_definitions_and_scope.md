# Definitions and scope

Residual stress is stress that remains after external tractions are removed. Residual strain is the deformation released after a cut or separation. Prestress is a broader term that may include externally maintained loading, active tone, deposition prestretch, growth incompatibility, or an initial-stress field.

These quantities must not be used interchangeably. An opening angle is a geometric observation produced by a release protocol; it is not itself a stress value. Converting it to stress requires a reference-configuration hypothesis, constitutive law, geometry, axial constraint, and equilibrium solution.

The tutorial covers tubes, multilayer walls, sectors, strips, plates, and solid-organ slices. Arteries are the classical example, but the same mechanics appears in veins, airways, intestine, myocardium, skin, tendon, growth plates, tumors, engineered constructs, and swelling gels.
