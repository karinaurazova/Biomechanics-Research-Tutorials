# Verification and validation hierarchy

Verification checks mathematics and code: radial equilibrium, boundary tractions, incompressibility, force and moment balance, convergence, positive Jacobians, and reproducibility.

Validation compares predictions with independent observations: released geometry, pressure–diameter curves, axial force, full-field strain, layer-specific opening, or spatial stress surrogates. Data used to fit an opening angle cannot independently validate the same quantity.

The benchmark in this tutorial is fully synthetic and verification-oriented. It is designed to catch implementation errors, not to establish biological truth.
