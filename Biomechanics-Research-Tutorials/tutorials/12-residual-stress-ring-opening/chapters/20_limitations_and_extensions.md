# Limitations and extensions

The core sector solver is axisymmetric, quasi-static, incompressible, and hyperelastic. It omits viscoelastic release, active tone, fluid transport, damage, noncircular anatomy, contact, branching, and three-dimensional cut propagation.

Extensions include anisotropic finite elements, inverse elastography, initial-stress finite elements, morphoelastic growth, layer-specific remodeling, poromechanics, uncertainty-aware calibration, and automated contour extraction.

The main educational lesson is that released geometry is evidence of incompatibility, but residual stress is a model-based reconstruction whose assumptions must remain visible.
