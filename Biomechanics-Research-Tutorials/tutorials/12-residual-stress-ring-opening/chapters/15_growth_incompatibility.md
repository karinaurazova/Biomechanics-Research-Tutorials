# Growth-induced incompatibility

In morphoelasticity, a local growth tensor can be incompatible: no global deformation realizes it without elastic accommodation. The elastic part then stores residual stress even when external traction is zero.

Differential circumferential growth can generate ring opening; through-thickness growth gradients can bend strips; spatially varying growth can create wrinkling or complex released shapes.

The tutorial connects a simple stress-modulated growth rule to an evolving opening angle while emphasizing that this is one possible mechanism, not a unique inverse interpretation.
