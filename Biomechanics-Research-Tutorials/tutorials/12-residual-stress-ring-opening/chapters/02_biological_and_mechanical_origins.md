# Biological and mechanical origins

Residual stress can arise from differential growth, heterogeneous turnover, deposition under load, maturation, cross-linking, active contraction, osmotic swelling, thermal mismatch, damage, or manufacturing prestretch. Several mechanisms may coexist.

From a mechanobiological perspective, residual stress can redistribute working stress, preserve patency, tune mechanosensory cues, alter stability, and store energy. It may be adaptive, neutral, or pathological depending on magnitude, direction, tissue, and loading state.

A release experiment reveals incompatibility but rarely identifies its origin. Mechanistic interpretation therefore requires complementary structural, temporal, biochemical, and mechanical evidence.
