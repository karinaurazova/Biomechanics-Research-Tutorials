# Strip and plate release

Residual strain gradients through thickness bend a released strip. In a reduced beam model, the compatible axial stretch is approximated by λ(z)=λ0+κz and λ0, κ are chosen so that resultant force and moment vanish.

This model applies to arterial strips, skin, myocardium slices, laminated tissues, growth plates, and engineered bilayers. It reveals residual bending that an annular opening test cannot measure.

The tutorial verifies force and moment balance and converts curvature to an equivalent opening measure only as a visualization aid.
