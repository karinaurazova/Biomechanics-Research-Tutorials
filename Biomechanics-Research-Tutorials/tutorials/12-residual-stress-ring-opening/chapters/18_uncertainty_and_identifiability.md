# Uncertainty and identifiability

Uncertainty enters through geometry, angle definition, material parameters, layer boundaries, axial stretch, active tone, and constitutive-model choice. Reporting only one reconstructed stress curve hides these sources.

Local sensitivity, Monte Carlo propagation, profile likelihood, and Bayesian inference are complementary tools. Structural non-identifiability cannot be fixed by reducing numerical noise.

The tutorial visualizes an angle–modulus loss surface and separates measurement uncertainty from model-form uncertainty.
