# Solid organs and non-tubular tissues

Residual stress is not restricted to tubes. Cuts in solid organs can gape, curl, twist, or change local thickness. Examples include myocardium, brain, tumor spheroids, cartilage, growth plates, and developing organs.

For non-tubular bodies, a single opening-angle model is usually inadequate. One may need incompatible metric fields, initial-stress formulations, morphoelastic finite elements, or full-field inverse methods.

The tutorial uses a synthetic slice and strip release to demonstrate which conclusions remain geometric and which require a spatial mechanics solver.
