# 2. Learning objectives and assessment

The tutorial uses observable learning outcomes and constructive alignment between objectives, activities, and evidence.

| Level | Learner action | Learning activity | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Explain | Describe axial orientation and 180-degree equivalence | Boundary-angle discussion | Short written explanation |
| Apply | Run the baseline simulation | Guided coding | Reproduced figure and parameter table |
| Analyse | Compare remodeling rates and time steps | Parameter study | Plot and quantitative adaptation-time metric |
| Evaluate | Identify ambiguity and model limitations | Orthogonal-case experiment | Structured critique |
| Create | Add a new cue or nonlinear kinetic law | Research Challenge | Tested extension and interpretation |

A successful completion requires more than producing the expected graph. The learner should explain why the result follows from the mathematical assumptions, distinguish verification from validation, and state what evidence would be needed before the law could be used as a biological model.

## Minimum mastery criteria

A learner has achieved the core outcomes when they can:

1. compute an axial mismatch without a discontinuity at 180 degrees;
2. explain why the exactly orthogonal case is non-unique;
3. reproduce the analytical relaxation for a fixed target;
4. demonstrate numerical convergence as the time step decreases;
5. report at least two limitations without claiming experimental validation.
