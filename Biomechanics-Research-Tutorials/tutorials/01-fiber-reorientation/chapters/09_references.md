# 9. References and evidence map

This tutorial cites primary research articles. The papers support the broader connection between mechanics and fibrous architecture; they do not validate the tutorial's minimal first-order law.

## Experimental motivation

1. **Sawhney and Howard (2002)** observed coordinated, cell-driven reorganization of collagen gels and quantified the development of anisotropy. DOI: [10.1083/jcb.200203069](https://doi.org/10.1083/jcb.200203069)
2. **Neidert and Tranquillo (2006)** used mechanical constraints during cell-mediated gel compaction to generate aligned collagen structures in tissue-engineered valve constructs. DOI: [10.1089/ten.2006.12.891](https://doi.org/10.1089/ten.2006.12.891)
3. **Weidenhamer and Tranquillo (2013)** showed that cyclic stretch and tissue constraints jointly affected cellular and collagen alignment in fibroblast-derived sheets. DOI: [10.1089/ten.tec.2012.0423](https://doi.org/10.1089/ten.tec.2012.0423)
4. **Matsugaki, Fujiwara, and Nakano (2013)** reported alignment of osteoblasts under cyclic stretch and anisotropic organization of subsequently formed collagen matrix. DOI: [10.1016/j.actbio.2013.03.015](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.actbio.2013.03.015)

## Computational precedent

5. **Driessen et al. (2003a)** analyzed mechanically induced collagen remodeling in the aortic valve using loading-related preferred directions. DOI: [10.1115/1.1590361](https://doi.org/10.1115/1.1590361)
6. **Driessen et al. (2003b)** represented a continuous collagen orientation distribution and evolving fiber content in soft connective tissue. DOI: [10.1016/S0021-9290(03)00082-4](https://doi.org/10.1016/S0021-9290(03)00082-4)
7. **Driessen et al. (2004)** modeled arterial collagen directions located between principal stretch directions and demonstrated coupling between architecture and mechanics. DOI: [10.1016/j.jtbi.2003.08.004](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jtbi.2003.08.004)
8. **Driessen, Bouten, and Baaijens (2005)** applied a preferred-direction remodeling hypothesis to the aortic valve and compared predicted architecture qualitatively with known leaflet organization. DOI: [10.1115/1.1865187](https://doi.org/10.1115/1.1865187)
9. **Hariton et al. (2007)** developed a stress-driven collagen fiber remodeling model for arterial walls. DOI: [10.1007/s10237-006-0049-7](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10237-006-0049-7)
10. **Driessen et al. (2008)** evolved the main direction and dispersity of angular collagen distributions in cardiovascular tissues under stress- or strain-related stimuli. DOI: [10.1007/s10237-007-0078-x](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10237-007-0078-x)

## Deliberate simplifications in this tutorial

Compared with the cited computational literature, the tutorial:

- prescribes the target direction;
- uses one effective orientation instead of multiple families or a distribution;
- holds fiber content and stiffness fixed;
- omits constitutive feedback between architecture and stress;
- uses dimensionless synthetic time unless a user supplies calibrated units.

Machine-readable entries are provided in [`references.bib`](../references.bib).
