# Homeostatic target, band, and surface

A scalar target \(S_h\) defines the normalized error

\[
e=\frac{S-S_h}{S_{\mathrm{scale}}}.
\]

Biological regulation may tolerate a range rather than enforce exact equality. A dead zone of half-width \(\delta\) gives

\[
\mathcal E(e)=\operatorname{sign}(e)\max(|e|-\delta,0).
\]

In multidimensional stress space the zero-response set is a homeostatic surface or band. For a mean-stress stimulus it is a plane normal to the hydrostatic axis, not a von-Mises cylinder. Calling both objects “the target stress” conceals fundamentally different predictions under multiaxial loading.

A homeostatic target is also not automatically a thermodynamic equilibrium. Living tissue can consume chemical energy to maintain a non-zero stress state. Hence the target must be interpreted as a mechanobiological set-point, while energetic admissibility is assessed separately.
