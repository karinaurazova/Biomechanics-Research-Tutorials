# Limitations and transition to Tutorial 09

The model is homogeneous or one-dimensionally local, isotropic in its growth kinematics, quasi-static in mechanics and based on a compressible neo-Hookean elastic response. It does not include nutrient transport, cell populations, constituent-specific turnover, finite-element equilibrium, damage, active contraction or experimental calibration.

Most importantly, a scalar \(J_g\) cannot represent fiber-family reorientation, directional collagen deposition or sheet-normal hypertrophy. Those processes require tensorial internal variables and separate directional stimuli.

Tutorial 09 therefore moves to fiber-family remodeling. The present module supplies the scalar feedback, numerical safeguards and verification logic that will be reused there.
