# Hydrostatic and deviatoric limitations

Isotropic volumetric growth has only one scalar degree of freedom. It can adapt the hydrostatic part of the elastic state, but it cannot independently control directional or shear distortions.

Under a fixed uniaxial total deformation, a mean-Mandel-driven law can bring

\[
\frac13\operatorname{tr}\mathbf M
\]

close to its target while the von Mises stress remains non-zero. The system is homeostatic with respect to the selected scalar stimulus but not stress-free.

This distinction is central. A successful scalar error trace does not prove that the full tensor state has been restored. Directional hypertrophy, fiber remodeling or anisotropic growth tensors are required when the biological objective involves directional stresses.
