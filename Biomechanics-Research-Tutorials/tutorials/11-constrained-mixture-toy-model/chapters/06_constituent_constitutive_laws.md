# Constituent constitutive laws

Each constituent needs an elastic law appropriate to its role and scale. The tutorial uses simple tension-only exponential fibers and an isotropic matrix so that cohort bookkeeping remains visible.

For real applications, one may replace these laws by neo-Hookean, Mooney–Rivlin, Holzapfel–Gasser–Ogden, Lanir-type angular integration, viscoelastic, active, damage, or poroelastic formulations. The constrained-mixture framework does not prescribe one constitutive law.

Compression cutoffs, recruitment, dispersion, and units must be documented because they can dominate inferred production rates.
