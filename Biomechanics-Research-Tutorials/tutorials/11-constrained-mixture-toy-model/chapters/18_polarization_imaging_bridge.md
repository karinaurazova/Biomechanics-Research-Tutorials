# Imaging-informed structural priors

Orientation-sensitive imaging can inform mean direction, directional order, layer boundaries, and relative structural contrast. Potential modalities include histology, structure-tensor microscopy, polarization imaging, diffusion imaging, optical coherence methods, and micro-CT.

The tutorial maps synthetic orientation, order, and contrast fields to bounded mixture priors. The mapping is intentionally generic and transparent. It does not identify turnover rates, deposition prestretch, constituent stiffness, or survival functions.

Imaging should therefore be treated as one part of a multimodal inverse problem, combined with mechanical tests and, when turnover is studied, temporal or biochemical observations.
