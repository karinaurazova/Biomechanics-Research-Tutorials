# Natural configurations and deposition stretch

A newly deposited constituent need not be stress free in the current mixture configuration. Deposition stretch records the elastic state assigned at birth and generates prestress and mechanical memory.

Different biological interpretations are possible: active deposition under cellular tension, assembly into an already loaded matrix, maturation-induced shortening, or fabrication prestretch in engineered materials. The parameter should therefore be linked to a clearly stated mechanism and configuration.

The tutorial shows that changing deposition stretch can alter stress without changing mass. Consequently composition measurements cannot replace mechanical information.
