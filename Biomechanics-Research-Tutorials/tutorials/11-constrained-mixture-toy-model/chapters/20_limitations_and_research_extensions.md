# Limitations and extensions

The implementation is a zero-dimensional teaching model. It omits spatial equilibrium, transport, fluid–solid coupling, active contraction, damage, cell migration, biochemical networks, and experimentally calibrated units.

Extensions include finite elements, multilayer geometry, multiple fiber distributions, poromechanics, chemical signaling, Bayesian inference, and data assimilation. Each extension should answer a specific question rather than merely increase model size.

The educational goal is to teach model choice: what is represented, what is averaged, what is observable, what is assumed, and what claim is justified by the available evidence.
