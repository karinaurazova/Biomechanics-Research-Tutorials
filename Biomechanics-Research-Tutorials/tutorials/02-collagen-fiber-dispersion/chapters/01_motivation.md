# 1. Motivation

A single fiber direction is an efficient abstraction, but histological and optical images of soft tissues rarely show perfectly parallel collagen. Tendons may exhibit a narrow distribution, while skin, arterial adventitia, valve leaflets, fascia, and healing matrices can contain broad, asymmetric, or multimodal orientation patterns.

This spread matters mechanically because a fiber carries load according to its angle relative to deformation. Replacing a distribution by only its mean direction can hide fibers that are already close to the loading axis or exaggerate the role of fibers that are not.

The tutorial asks three linked questions:

1. How can axial orientation dispersion be represented statistically?
2. How is the distribution summarized by an orientation tensor?
3. How does the complete distribution influence a nonlinear fiber contribution to stress?

The aim is not to reproduce one tissue dataset. The aim is to create a transparent bridge from image-derived orientation histograms to structural constitutive modeling.
