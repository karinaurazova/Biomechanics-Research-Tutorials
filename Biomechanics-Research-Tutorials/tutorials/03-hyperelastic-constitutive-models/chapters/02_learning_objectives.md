# 02 — Learning objectives

By the end of the module, the learner should be able to:

- **remember and define** the tensors \(\mathbf F\), \(\mathbf C\), \(\bar{\mathbf C}\), and the Jacobian \(J\);
- **calculate** isochoric invariants and principal stretches;
- **implement** energy functions based on invariants, principal stretches, and structural tensors;
- **compare** the predictions of several models under uniaxial, biaxial, plane-strain, shear, and volumetric paths;
- **verify** a numerical derivative against a closed-form Neo-Hookean response;
- **analyse** sensitivity to limiting-chain, Ogden, fiber-angle, and dispersion parameters;
- **evaluate** whether a chosen experimental protocol is sufficient for parameter identification;
- **design** a small independent model-comparison study with explicit assumptions and limitations.
