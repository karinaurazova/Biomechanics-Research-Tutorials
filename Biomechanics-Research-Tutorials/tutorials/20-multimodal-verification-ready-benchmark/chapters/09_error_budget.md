# Stage-wise error budget

The error budget separates segmentation, orientation and inverse-mechanics metrics. This makes it possible to identify whether the limiting stage is the image, the structural reconstruction, the DIC/force observation or the inverse problem.

Best Dice does not automatically mean best mechanics. A mask with slightly lower Dice may preserve continuity better and produce more accurate parameters.

The budget is a teaching map, not a final ranking. A real study should add repeated seeds, uncertainty intervals and additional topology or boundary metrics.

The chapter should be read together with the code. Every quantity mentioned here is created explicitly in `multimodal_benchmark.py` and regenerated by `reproduce.py`. The purpose is not to hide complexity behind a black box, but to show which assumptions enter the pipeline.

For interpretation, always ask four questions: what is the hidden truth, what is observed, what is reconstructed, and which metric compares them? This four-part logic prevents a visually appealing result from being mistaken for a verified mechanical conclusion.

## Implementation details

The error budget is intentionally compact. It records the best segmentation Dice, best orientation mean absolute error and best relative parameter error. These are not the only important values, but they provide a quick map of the pipeline. More detailed values remain available in the individual CSV files.

## What to check while reading

Use `error_budget.csv` as a starting point, not an endpoint. If the parameter error is high, go backward: check the oracle scenario, then orientation, then segmentation. This reverse tracing is a practical debugging method for image-informed biomechanics.

## Typical misunderstanding

A single final score would be convenient, but it would hide too much. Different stages have different scientific meanings. A segmentation score, an orientation score and a parameter score should not be collapsed unless the weighting is justified by a specific scientific question.
