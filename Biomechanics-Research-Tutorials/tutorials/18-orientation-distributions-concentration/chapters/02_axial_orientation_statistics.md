# Axial orientation statistics

A fibre axis has no arrow. The angles `theta` and `theta + pi` represent the same physical direction. For this reason, means and concentration parameters are computed by doubling the angles, applying circular statistics, and then halving the mean phase.

The mean resultant length `R` is close to zero for broadly distributed orientations and close to one for highly aligned fibres. The same quantity is also related to the anisotropy of the second-order orientation tensor.
