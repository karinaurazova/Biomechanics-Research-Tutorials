# Multiple families, crossings, and ambiguity

Many tissues and engineered membranes contain two or more directional families. Tutorial 13 stores a family identifier on every fiber and produces a dominant-family raster in addition to a single-axis orientation field.

At a true crossing, one orientation angle cannot encode both directions. The coherence field quantifies the cancellation of doubled-angle moments but does not identify the missing directions. This distinction prepares the benchmark for later comparisons between structure tensors, orientation distributions, and instance segmentation.

Family separation, relative density, radius, and waviness can be varied independently.
