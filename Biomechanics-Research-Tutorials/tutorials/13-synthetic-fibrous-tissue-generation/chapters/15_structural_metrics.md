# Porosity, thickness, connectivity, and descriptors

Tutorial 13 reports fiber count, total contour length, mean length, area fraction, porosity, mean axial orientation, order parameter, mean local thickness, component count, endpoints, branch pixels, and largest-component fraction.

No single descriptor characterizes a network. Two structures can have the same porosity and different alignment, connectivity, pore geometry, or defect population. Conversely, different random realizations can share statistical descriptors while differing locally.

Later constitutive and homogenization tutorials will use these descriptors as inputs or explanatory variables, but mechanical sufficiency must be tested rather than assumed.
