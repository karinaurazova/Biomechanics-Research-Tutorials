# Branches, gaps, and controlled defects

Branches are added at interior polyline points with a controlled probability, length, and angle. Defects are introduced either by deleting complete fibers or by splitting a fiber around an internal gap.

The defect mechanism remains explicit in metadata. This is preferable to hiding damage inside generic image noise because segmentation failure and structural discontinuity are different error sources.

Controlled defects enable sensitivity studies for connectivity, skeleton continuity, segmentation metrics, and later network mechanics.
