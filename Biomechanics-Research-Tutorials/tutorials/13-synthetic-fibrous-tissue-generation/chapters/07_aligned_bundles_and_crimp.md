# Aligned bundles, crimp, and tortuosity

Parallel bundles are generated by placing long fibers at approximately regular normal offsets. Small angular jitter prevents an unrealistically perfect lattice. Sinusoidal waviness controls contour-to-chord ratio, a geometric measure of tortuosity.

Crimp is kept separate from stiffness. A wavier fiber can show delayed recruitment in a later mechanical model, but Tutorial 13 only creates geometry. Assigning a constitutive law belongs to image-informed mechanics tutorials.

The bundle preset is useful for testing orientation algorithms because its global axis is known, while local tangents remain spatially variable.
