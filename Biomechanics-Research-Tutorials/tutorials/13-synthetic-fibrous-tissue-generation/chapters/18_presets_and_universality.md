# Presets and universal educational scope

Presets provide quick access to aligned, crossed, layered, rotating, cellular, and nonwoven architectures. Their names indicate a qualitative analogy, not an organ-specific assertion.

Users should construct new presets by composing transparent generators and documenting which descriptors are controlled. A universal repository benefits from families of architectures rather than a single preferred tissue.

The same ground-truth schema allows subsequent imaging and mechanics pipelines to operate on any preset without changing their interfaces.
